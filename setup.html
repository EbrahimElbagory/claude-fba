// 00 — API CREDENTIALS

Get your Amazon API keys.

Claude FBA talks to Amazon through your own private SP-API app — no third-party service ever sees your data, and credentials never leave your machine. Creating the app is free, needs no approval from Amazon beyond your own seller account, and takes about 30 minutes once.

TIME: ~30 MINCOST: $0 REQUIRES: PRO SELLER ACCOUNTAPPROVAL WAIT: NONE*
01Register

Become a developer (your own account)

In Seller Central go to Apps & Services → Develop Apps. If you've never used it, Amazon asks you to complete a Developer Profile first: choose Private developer (you're building for your own business), answer the data-use questionnaire honestly (analytics/automation for your own store; you're not touching customer PII), and submit.

*The one wait that can happenPrivate developer profiles are usually approved instantly or within a day or two. If yours goes to review, everything below waits on it — do this step first, today.
02Create app

Add a new app client

  1. Develop Apps → Add new app client
  2. App name: anything (claude-fba works) · API type: SP-API
  3. Check the roles the toolkit uses:
Product ListingREQUIRED
read + patch listings, A+ read
Inventory & Order TrackingREQUIRED
orders + review solicitations
PricingREQUIRED
price data
Selling Partner InsightsREQUIRED
account/marketplace info
Brand AnalyticsOPTIONAL
search query performance

Save. The app appears in your Develop Apps list in Draft — that's fine; self-authorization works from draft.

03LWA keys

Copy your LWA credentials

In the app row click View under LWA credentials. Copy both values somewhere safe:

  • Client ID — looks like amzn1.application-oa2-client.…
  • Client Secret — long string. Treat it like a password.
SecurityThese credentials give API access to your store. Never commit them, never paste them into websites or chats, never share screenshots of this screen. Claude FBA keeps them in a gitignored local file only.
04Authorize

Self-authorize → refresh token

Back in the Develop Apps list: your app row → Authorize → confirm. Amazon shows a Refresh Token starting with Atzr|. Copy it immediately (you can regenerate later, but a new token invalidates the old one).

This token is what lets the toolkit act as you, on your store only. There's no OAuth dance with third parties because there are none.

05Configure

Wire it into Claude FBA

cp scripts/config.example.env scripts/config.env

Open scripts/config.env and fill in the three values from steps 03–04: LWA_CLIENT_ID, LWA_CLIENT_SECRET, SP_REFRESH_TOKEN.

06Verify

Prove it works

python3 scripts/sp_api.py

This exchanges your token and probes all three SP-API regions, printing every marketplace your account sells in. Copy the region and marketplace ID it reports into config.env (SP_REGION, MARKETPLACE_ID) and you're done — every skill and script in the toolkit works from here.

SymptomCause & fix
403 Unauthorized on one endpointApp is missing that role. Edit the app in Develop Apps, check the role, and re-authorize — role changes require a fresh refresh token.
invalid_grant on token exchangeThe refresh token was rotated (e.g. someone clicked Authorize again). Grab the newest token from the app row.

Develop Apps not visibleYou need a Professional selling plan, and the Developer Profile from step 01 must be approved.
